When briefing a reviewer for a task that makes one especially consequential, hard-to-reverse, or externally-visible claim, explicitly flag that ONE claim as deserving disproportionate scrutiny and multiple independent verification angles -- rather than listing it as one bullet among a flat checklist of equally-weighted items. The review's depth on any given item tends to track how the brief framed its stakes, not just whether it was mentioned at all.
