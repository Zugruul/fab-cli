Recall is for knowledge discovery, NOT for guaranteeing procedure compliance: a subagent consulting a brain-backed identity skipped protocol steps minted into that brain EARLIER THE SAME SESSION (the fresh note hadn't settled into links/activation, so recall never surfaced it), and the orchestrator had to close the loop manually. Rule: when delegating a consult to an identity, NAME the operative protocol note in the delegation prompt — one line ("follow card-interaction-protocol in full") costs nothing and makes compliance explicit. Freshly-minted protocols especially: until a protocol appears in the role's ROLE.md or has accumulated link weight, same-session consults will not reliably find it. Cf. [[lean-vs-briefed-identity-validation]], [[recall-needs-structural-links]].
