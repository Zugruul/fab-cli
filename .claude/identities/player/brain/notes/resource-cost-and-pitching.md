Most cards have a resource cost (top-right pip) you must pay to play them (learn-to-play transcript; CR 1.14). You generate resource points by PITCHING cards from hand: move a card from hand to the pitch zone, gaining resource points equal to its pitch value (CR 1.14.3, 1.13.3a). You may only pitch when you actually need the assets to pay a cost, or when an effect tells you to (CR 1.14.3b). Pitched cards go to the bottom of your deck at end of turn (CR 4.4.3c) — see [[second-cycle-awareness]]. The tension: pitching a card spends it for resources instead of playing it, so which cards you pitch vs play is the core of [[pitch-curve-planning]] and [[hand-value-card-advantage]].
