In the Damage Step, if the attack's power is GREATER than the sum of all defending cards' defense values, the defender takes damage equal to the difference (CR 7.5.2; learn-to-play transcript). Example: 6 power vs 3+2 defense = 1 damage through. If power does not exceed total defense, zero damage and the attack does not hit. Dealing this physical damage is a "hit" (CR 7.5.5), which matters because many effects trigger only "when this hits" (e.g. draw/disrupt on hit). So blocking to exactly negate an attack also denies its on-hit effect — often more valuable than the life saved. Three damage types exist: generic, physical ({p}, from combat), and arcane (from effects) (CR 7.5, 2.5). Arcane damage bypasses normal blocking and is stopped differently ([[keyword-damage-prevention]]). See [[block-vs-take-tradeoff]], [[defending-from-hand]].
