Don't overrate **Betsy, Skin in the Game**'s overpower as a "protect my attack from a second blocker" tool — by the time an attack wagers (CR 8.5.46/8.5.46a), the defending cards are already locked in for that chain link, so the {r}{r} payment for overpower can't retroactively strip a second action-card defender (CR 8.3.22a/b — same non-retroactive rule that protects dominate/overpower grants everywhere). The real value of Betsy's overpower grant is downstream: it matters for anything ELSE checking "does this attack have overpower" later in the same combat chain (other card text, or if the attack somehow re-enters a defend step via an unusual effect), not for the wagering attack's own block count. Sequence your {r}{r} payment as an EV/value add-on (+1{p} plus a flag for later checks), not as removal insurance.

See [[kw-wager]], [[kw-overpower]].
