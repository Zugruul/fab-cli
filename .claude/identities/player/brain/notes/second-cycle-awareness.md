You almost always cycle through your entire deck at least once per game (learn-to-play transcript). Pitched cards go to the BOTTOM of your deck in an order you choose (CR 4.4.3c, 1.14.3), so the cards you pitch now are the cards you will draw later — in roughly the order you pitched them. Two consequences for strong play: (1) pitch-stack intentionally — if you know you want a certain card back sooner, order your pitch pile accordingly; (2) do not pitch a key card you will need before the deck cycles back around. This "you play with what you pitch" awareness (transcript) turns pitching from a throwaway into deck manipulation. Blocking, by contrast, sends cards to the graveyard, out of the cycle ([[chain-closing]]). Built on [[resource-cost-and-pitching]] and [[pitch-curve-planning]].
