The central FAB resource is not mana or life but your hand, refilled to intellect each turn ([[intellect-and-hand-refill]]). Because you redraw to a fixed hand size regardless of how many cards you had left, the real unit of value is "cards used per turn": a card played, pitched, or blocked with is value extracted; a card sitting in hand at end of turn is value lost. Card advantage in FAB therefore means net cards ahead in an exchange — e.g. trading your 1 attack for the opponent's 2 blocks puts you up. Attacking to force multi-card blocks, or attacks that draw on hit, generate card advantage; over-blocking or whiffing loses it. This is why FAB is often described as a game of resource attrition. Directly built on [[resource-cost-and-pitching]] and [[block-vs-take-tradeoff]]; realized through [[pitch-curve-planning]] and [[tempo-and-go-again-chains]].
