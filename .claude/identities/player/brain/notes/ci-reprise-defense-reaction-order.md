**Ironsong Response**'s Reprise bonus (+3{p} to a weapon attack if the defending hero defended with a hand card) is checked ONCE, at the moment Ironsong Response itself resolves (CR 8.4.3a) — not continuously, and not retroactively. If your opponent declared no hand-card defenders in the Defend Step, don't assume the swing is dead: play Ironsong Response anyway and see if they're holding a Defense Reaction (like Beneath the Surface) to protect their hero. Because Defense Reaction cards resolve as defending cards the moment they resolve (CR 8.1.3b), and the stack resolves last-in-first-out, if your opponent responds to your Ironsong Response by casting their own Defense Reaction, THEIR card resolves first — which flips your Reprise condition true right before your own ability checks it, netting you the +3{p} for free off their own defensive play.

Conversely, if you're the defending player holding a Defense Reaction against a Reprise attack reaction, do NOT play it in response to their Reprise card if you can avoid it — responding hands them the bonus. Wait until after their reaction fully resolves (or play your Defense Reaction on a different, non-Reprise-linked attack reaction) so your hand-card defense never becomes visible to their Reprise check in time.

See [[kw-attack-reaction]], [[kw-defense-reaction]].
