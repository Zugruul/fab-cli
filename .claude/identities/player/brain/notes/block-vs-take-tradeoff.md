The recurring FAB decision: block an incoming attack (spending cards from hand as defense, CR 7.3) or take the damage and keep those cards. Blocking costs cards permanently — blockers go to the graveyard at chain close ([[chain-closing]]), not back into your deck — but preserves life and can deny on-hit triggers ([[hit-determination-and-damage]]). Taking damage preserves your cards for offense/pitching but spends life, which is finite ([[objective-and-life]]). Rules of thumb the fundamentals imply: block to deny painful on-hit effects; take small chip damage when your cards are worth more on offense; use your worst offensive cards (often blues you would pitch anyway) as blocks. This tension is the heart of tempo vs attrition. See [[hand-value-card-advantage]], [[defending-from-hand]], [[pitch-values-and-color]].
