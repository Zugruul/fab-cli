**Break Tide**'s Combo clause (gains dominate if Rushing River/Flood of Force was the prior attack) is a state-based static bonus, not a one-time trigger — it's live the instant Break Tide becomes attacking, so the opponent already sees "dominate" when deciding how to defend and can only commit ONE hand card in response (CR 8.3.4a).

Sequencing lesson: dominate never retroactively strips defenders (CR 8.3.4c) — so there is zero value in trying to "sneak" a dominate-granting effect in AFTER your opponent has already committed 2 hand cards to defend; it does nothing to cards already on the chain link. The leverage window for a combo-granted dominate attack is entirely BEFORE the opponent commits their first defending card. Build your combo sequencing (Rushing River/Flood of Force → Break Tide) so the dominate threat is visible at the moment of the defend decision, not after.
