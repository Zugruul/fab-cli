After blocks are declared, the Reaction Step opens (CR 7.4). Only reaction cards can be played here: the attacker may play attack reactions (CR 8.1.2a), the defender may play defense reactions (CR 8.1.3a). These are the "combat tricks" (learn-to-play transcript) — e.g. an attack reaction that pumps power, or a defense reaction that adds defense. Reactions are instants in timing: they do NOT cost an action point (see [[action-points-and-go-again]]). A defense reaction, when it resolves, becomes a defending card on the chain link (CR 7.4.2d, 8.1.3b), and it may be played from arsenal ([[arsenal]]). Reactions resolve on the stack, so they can be responded to. Holding a reaction is real information advantage — the threat of an unknown trick changes how the opponent commits. See [[chain-link-steps]], [[hit-determination-and-damage]].
