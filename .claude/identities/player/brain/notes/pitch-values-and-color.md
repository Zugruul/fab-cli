Every deck-card has a pitch value shown by a colored strip and socketed resource pips (learn-to-play transcript): blue = 3 resource, yellow = 2, red = 1. Typical play pattern: pitch blue (high-resource, usually lower-impact) cards to pay for higher-impact yellow and red cards. Color is also part of card identity — a card is "unique" by name AND color, so Sink Below (red) and Sink Below (blue) are different cards for deckbuilding limits (see [[format-classic-constructed]]). Blue cards often double as good blocks (see [[block-vs-take-tradeoff]]) since they tend to have solid defense but you would rather pitch than play them. Feeds [[resource-cost-and-pitching]] and [[pitch-curve-planning]].
