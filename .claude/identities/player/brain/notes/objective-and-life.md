Flesh and Blood is a 1v1 fight to the death: you win when your opponent's hero life total is reduced to zero, and you lose if yours hits zero or you do not control a hero (CR 4.5.2, 4.5.3a). Each hero starts with a printed life total and a printed intellect (hand size). Life is a resource, not just a clock — you can spend it blocking damage or (for some cards) paying life costs, so "life is a resource to be spent, not hoarded" is a core FAB mindset. Every point of damage you take is permanent unless an effect heals it. This frames every decision: is this block/trade worth the life, cards, and tempo? See [[block-vs-take-tradeoff]], [[hit-determination-and-damage]], [[intellect-and-hand-refill]]. Formats change starting life (e.g. [[format-classic-constructed]] vs [[format-silver-age]]).
