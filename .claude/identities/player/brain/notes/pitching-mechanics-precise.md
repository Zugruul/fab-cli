Precise pitch rules that win games (CR 1.14): you may only pitch while paying a cost, and only cards that gain assets you still NEED (1.14.3b — can't pitch a resource-only card toward a chi cost). Pitch one at a time: once the cost is fully paid you must STOP (1.14.2d example — pitching blue into a 3-cost after having 1{r} banks 1 leftover; you can't add more). Chi is spent before resources on resource costs (1.14.2d). Multi-type costs pay in order: chi → resources → life → action points (1.14.2a). Leftover resource points persist in your pool for later that turn (transcript: Edge of Autumn example). Zero costs still count as costs (1.14.5). Cards without a pitch property can't be pitched at all (1.14.3a). Pitching is an event — it can be triggered on and replaced (1.14.3c).
