Because you can only pitch cards to gain the assets you actually need (CR 1.14.3b), and blue=3 / yellow=2 / red=1 ([[pitch-values-and-color]]), a good hand needs a workable "pitch curve": enough blue/high-pitch cards to fund your reds/yellows. A hand of four red high-cost cards may be unplayable (nothing to pitch); a hand of four blues has resources but little punch. Deckbuilders tune the ratio of pitch colors so average hands function. In play, plan the whole turn before pitching: identify which card funds which, since pitched cards leave your hand for the turn and return only on the next cycle ([[second-cycle-awareness]]). Over-pitching wastes cards you could have played or blocked with ([[hand-value-card-advantage]]). Related: [[resource-cost-and-pitching]], [[card-anatomy]].
