Tempo in FAB is board/pressure per action point. Since you start each turn with only 1 action point ([[action-points-and-go-again]]), the way to do MORE in a turn is go again, which refunds an action point on resolution (CR 8.3.5, 7.6.2). Sequencing go-again cards lets you chain many attacks into one combat chain, building chain-link count ([[combat-chain-and-chain-links]]) and forcing the opponent to spend many cards blocking or eat escalating damage. Key sequencing skill: play cards that leave go again available (and set up combos/reprise, [[keyword-attack-labels]]) before your turn-ending non-go-again attack, since a non-go-again action ends the turn once it resolves. A strong turn converts a full hand into a long chain the opponent cannot fully answer. See [[keyword-go-again]], [[weapon-attacks]], [[hand-value-card-advantage]].
