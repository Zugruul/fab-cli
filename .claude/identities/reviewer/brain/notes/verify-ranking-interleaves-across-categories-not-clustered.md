When grading a 'ranked findings' section that combines multiple sub-categories, don't just check that SOME order exists -- check that the ordering INTERLEAVES across categories. A genuine comparative ranking places findings from the same category apart from each other (effort x impact weighed against every other finding, not just restated in category order). If every category's findings cluster together in the rank list, that's the tell it's a restatement dressed as a ranking, not a real cross-category comparison.
