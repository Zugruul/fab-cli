A PR that 'corrects' its own source material (a design doc's citation, an earlier task's stated finding) deserves MORE scrutiny than an uncorroborated original claim, not less -- the correction itself is just as much an unverified claim as what it's replacing. Verify it the same way you'd verify any other citation (re-run the greps/checks yourself), even though a self-correction reads as inherently more credible ('they found their own mistake, so they must be careful'). In this case the correction happened to be right, but that was only established by independent verification, not by the narrative's self-aware framing.
