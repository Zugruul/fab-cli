The PPG applies to Competitive and Professional REL (Casual uses its own guide — see [[casual-rel-procedures]]) (PPG §1.1). A judge is an impartial arbiter who addresses infractions by applying procedures and issuing penalties, educating players, and equalizing advantage.

Three guiding principles behind every ruling (§1.1):
- Education: the player(s) understand what happened, why it's an infraction, and how it's addressed.
- Equity: remove any advantage gained. The PPG names four advantage types — information (private state a player shouldn't know), state (a more favorable game state), strategic (info influencing future decisions), and tournament (an artificial standing gain).
- Mission: balance education and equity against "playing great games" — casual leans to gentle/liberal education, competitive/professional to strict procedures for fairness.

No document covers every case, so judges are empowered to deviate (with Head Judge approval) — see [[deviations]]. Ruling within the guide also protects the judge: complaints go to the policy, not the person (§1.2). These principles frame the whole penalty system: [[penalty-severity-ladder]], [[fixing-game-states]], [[handling-an-infraction-workflow]]. — PPG (latest, 2026-06-10).
