General rule: players may NOT take notes during games (TRP §5.8). They may take notes between games; spectators may note any time, but those notes can't be referenced during a game (§5.8, §5.9).

Allowed during a game (§5.8) — must be clear, accurate, available to all: recording hero life totals and changes (and the source of a change); markers for turn-player/phase/combat-step; dice for remaining resource/action points, attack power, effect damage, count of specific cards in a PUBLIC zone, X values, or a card named by an effect (e.g. Chain of Eminence).

NOT allowed (§5.8): the current turn NUMBER; the order of pitched cards; opponent's revealed cards; cards played by either player; sorting graveyard/banished to track deck contents (except cards relevant to the current state); calculating future states in writing; tracking cards drawn or deck size; markers on the deck to remind you of start-of-turn triggers; a stale/mislabeled life pad.

Outside assistance (§5.9): no advice from spectators, no personal notes made before/during the event during a match/draft. EXCEPTION: personal notes and electronic devices may be used during the start-of-game procedure (e.g. sideboard guides), if not used to seek spectator advice. Silence expected during draft at Competitive/Professional.

Violations = Note Taking and Outside Assistance (PPG §3.2, Warning; upgrade to Game Loss if strategic advantage gained), see [[tournament-infractions-overview]]. Accessibility exceptions: [[game-information-categories]]. — TRP/PPG (latest, 2026-06-10).
