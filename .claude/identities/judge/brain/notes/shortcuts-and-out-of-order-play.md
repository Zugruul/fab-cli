Shortcuts (TRP §5.2): actions taken to skip tedious play by shared understanding. Valid only if all players mutually understand the shortcut and the resulting state. Most skip priority passes; skipping an opponent's DECISIONS (beyond passing priority) must be explicitly agreed. Any player may interrupt a shortcut (even their own) before all acknowledge the result; play resumes from the earliest interruption point. Shortcuts may not exploit poor communication or create ambiguity.

Out-of-order play / OOP (§5.3): executing actions not strictly correctly sequenced. Legal only if every action is legal, the end state is correct and clearly understood as if done in order, and no action used info that wouldn't have been available in the correct order. A player may ask the actor to redo in correct order to respond at the right time. A player may NOT modify their OOP based on the opponent's reaction, nor use OOP to retroactively take a missed action. A substantial pause signals the OOP is complete.

Reversing actions (§5.4): players commit to actions; no "taksies backsies" of a communicated (verbal or non-verbal) action. Dexterity errors aren't actions. A judge MAY allow a change only if certain no information was gained since — if unsure, don't allow it.

These bound what looks like an infraction vs. legal shorthand; missed windows tie to [[missed-trigger-timing-windows]] and [[priority-and-the-stack]]. — TRP (latest, 2026-06-10).
