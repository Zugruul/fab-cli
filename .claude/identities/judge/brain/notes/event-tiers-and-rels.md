FAB events comprise one or more tournaments (TRP §1.1). Two orthogonal axes a judge must know:

Tiers (1–4): the event's tier is the highest tier of any of its tournaments (§1.1). Tier 1–2 can be run by any TO with a GEM account (some need an LSS invite); Tier 3–4 are run by LSS or appointed Organized Play partners (§1.1).

Rules Enforcement Levels — three (TRP §1.2), each ruled differently in the PPG:
- Casual: local in-store play; emphasis on player education and enjoyment. Ruled from the Casual Procedure Guide, see [[casual-rel-procedures]].
- Competitive: balances education/enjoyment with tournament integrity.
- Professional: highest level; emphasis on tournament integrity.
A tournament is usually a single REL, but some shift (e.g. a Calling going Competitive → Professional on day 2) (§1.2).

REL drives severity: e.g. deck-checks are required at Professional, recommended at Competitive (TRP §3.8), and the PPG's Competitive/Professional procedures replace the casual ones. Ties to [[tournament-roles]] and [[penalty-severity-ladder]]. — TRP (latest, 2026-06-10).
