Casual REL uses the Flesh and Blood Casual Procedure Guide (dated 2023-10-13), NOT the PPG (Casual Guide, p.i). "Great games" here means friendly, lighthearted, social play; emphasis on education while keeping games fair. Players are encouraged to resolve infractions by mutual agreement before calling a judge; if no judge, the TO fills the role.

Key difference: there are NO Warnings or Intellect Penalties at Casual. Minor infractions → fix the game state + educate/caution. Major infractions → Game/Match Loss, or Disqualification as a last resort. If the event has decklists, high stakes, or players wanting more competition, upgrade the REL to Competitive.

Recommended casual fixes (Casual Guide):
- Game rules error: have them take a forgotten simple action; else rewind if caught immediately; else partial-fix and continue.
- Missed trigger: optional → assume declined; else if no critical decisions were made on its absence and it isn't disruptive, put it on the stack; otherwise let play proceed without it.
- Seeing/over-drawing cards: shuffle into a random portion if the deck is random, else reveal and leave / return known cards to top.
- Illegal deck: remove/insert cards, Cracked Baubles to reach minimum.
- Tardiness: <10 min → equal extra time; 10+ min → match loss.
- Serious misconduct (abuse, intentional cheating, theft, ignoring officials) → disqualify.

Contrast the stricter Competitive/Professional model in [[ppg-philosophy]] and [[penalty-severity-ladder]]; REL choice in [[event-tiers-and-rels]]. — Casual Procedure Guide (2023-10-13).
