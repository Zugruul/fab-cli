Every person at a FAB tournament holds one or more roles (TRP §2.1). Tournament officials = TO, Head Judge, Floor Judge, Scorekeeper, Game Attendant. Judge roles = Head Judge + Floor Judge.

- Player: knows the rules, brings materials, keeps a clear/legal game state, calls a judge for infractions/disputes (§2.2).
- Spectator: silent and passive; may only discreetly alert a judge to a violation (§2.3). At Competitive/Professional REL a spectator may not call a judge (§3.9).
- Tournament Organizer (TO): logistics, venue, staff, prizes, reporting results to LSS; handles player-conduct issues (§2.4).
- Head Judge: oversees the tournament, final ruling on appeals, final authority on card properties and layout/marked-card thresholds — see [[head-judge-authority]] (§2.5).
- Floor Judge: first responder to judge calls, answers questions, settles disputes, issues rulings/penalties, pauses games to investigate (§2.6).
- Scorekeeper: pairings, results entry, standings (§2.7).

Role limits by tier: for Tier 1 (except Skirmish), officials may also be players and the TO may judge; for Tier 2+, officials may not be players and the TO may not judge (§2.1). See [[event-tiers-and-rels]] and [[judge-calls-and-appeals]]. — TRP (latest, 2026-06-10).
