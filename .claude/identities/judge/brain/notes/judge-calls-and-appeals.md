A player may call a judge any time during their match — pause, raise a hand, call "Judge!" (TRP §3.9). Officials may pause and call on players' behalf. At Competitive/Professional REL spectators may NOT call a judge but may discreetly flag a game.

When a player should call immediately (§3.9): an unresolvable minor infraction, an infraction needing a corrective procedure or creating significant advantage, an unresolvable dispute, an emergency, needing to leave the table, or otherwise needing judge oversight. Players may also call for assisted information — see [[game-information-categories]]. During a call, players must answer all questions completely and honestly, and may ask to do so away from the table.

Responder order: prefer a Floor Judge first; a Head Judge responds if no Floor Judge is available in reasonable time (§3.9). Players may NOT request a specific judge or a different judge than the first responder. Translators are allowed if logistically possible.

Appeals (§3.9): after a judge rules but BEFORE the procedure/penalty is applied, either player may appeal to the Head Judge, whose ruling is final. If the HJ was the first responder, they should consult another judge before affirming/modifying — see [[head-judge-authority]]. Ignoring a judge's instruction is Unsporting Conduct (a player may appeal first) (PPG §4.1). Ties to [[tournament-roles]]. — TRP (latest, 2026-06-10).
