**Construct Nitro Mechanoid** (Mechanologist Action Construct): "Transform target Mechanologist head, chest, arms, legs, weapon and 3 Hyper Drivers you control into Nitro Mechanoid. If you don't, negate this. Go again." Negate (CR 8.5.26): "To negate a layer, the layer is cleared from the stack and it does not resolve." CR 8.5.26a: "If a layer negates itself while resolving, it does not finish resolving."

Ruling: if the transform cost cannot be paid (missing the required Mechanologist pieces or 3 Hyper Drivers) and the card's own resolution ability negates itself, CR 8.5.26a governs: the layer "does not finish resolving." Go again (CR 8.3.5) is itself a resolution ability that grants an action point "after all other resolution abilities have resolved" (8.3.5a) — but if the object's OWN resolution ability negates itself mid-resolution, the layer never finishes resolving, so any remaining resolution abilities on that same layer (including its own Go Again) do not occur either. Practical result: failing to meet the transform cost on Construct Nitro Mechanoid costs the action point too — it is not "negate the transform, still get the action point."
