Tournaments are split into rounds (TRP §3.1). Swiss rounds pair by standings/history/format; elimination rounds knock out match losers (§3.1). Typical structure: timed Swiss for all players, then untimed single-elimination top-8 (top-cut) (§3.1). Recommended round counts live in TRP Appendix A.2.

Match length: generally only 1 game wins a match unless the TO announces otherwise before the tournament (§3.1). Timed rounds are best-of-time; untimed rounds are best-of-N (§3.1).

Timing rules judges enforce (§3.2):
- The timer starts when the vast majority of players are seated; players may do the start-of-game procedure before the timer but can't take the first turn until it starts.
- If a judge pauses a match >1 minute, grant at least equal extra time. For a regular deck-check, grant the deck-check time plus 3 minutes to reshuffle/present, capped at the original round length — see [[deck-checks]].
- Feature matches get extra time or separate timing (§3.11).

Time extensions are also required whenever a ruling takes over a minute, rounded up (PPG §1.2). End-of-round resolution: [[end-of-game-procedure]]. Ties to [[event-tiers-and-rels]]. — TRP (latest, 2026-06-10).
