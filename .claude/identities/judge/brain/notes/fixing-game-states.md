Procedures restore game-state integrity; use only ones relevant to the infraction — never an irrelevant procedure as a de-facto penalty (PPG §1.3). The toolkit:
- Rewind: revert to a previous legal state (CR basis in [[game-state-actions-and-death]]). A SIMPLE rewind undoes the last action(s) for infractions caught immediately; a FULL rewind undoes many actions. Don't attempt a full rewind if you can't guarantee reconstructing the prior state or if it would reveal extra information. Rewind removes state advantage but can't erase information advantage.
- Partial fix: artificially adjust the current state (correct a life total; make a legal choice that should have been made; move a card to its correct zone if still known to all and non-disruptive; add/remove/move a counter; reveal a card that should have been shown; make a player lose if they should have). Don't partial-fix if a strategic decision was likely made on the infraction's result or it's too complex.
- Cards to top/bottom of deck: opponent-directed placement; balances information/strategic advantage when reshuffling isn't practical (FAB decks are rarely randomized).
- Shuffle into a random portion: fully removes advantage but rarely usable because FAB deck order is mostly non-random — use only as a deviation when appropriate.

Effects only re-trigger from a partial fix if they would have triggered at the correct time (§1.3). Choice of fix is per-infraction in [[gameplay-infractions-overview]]. Severity in [[penalty-severity-ladder]]. — PPG (latest, 2026-06-10).
