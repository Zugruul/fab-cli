Intentional draws: players may mutually agree to draw games/matches at any time EXCEPT in elimination rounds (TRP §3.5). An elimination match that would draw goes to the [[end-of-game-procedure]] tiebreak.

Concessions (§3.5): allowed any time except in timed rounds — in a timed round a player may concede up to and including when time expires, but once they take (or acknowledge the opponent's) an in-game action after that point they may no longer concede and must play to a natural conclusion. Recommend calling a judge to concede. A player who refuses to play is treated as conceding; if both refuse, both lose.

Hard limits a judge enforces:
- No concession in exchange for consideration — that's Bribery (TRP §6.4, PPG §4.5).
- A player may NOT ask for or pressure a concession at any time; implying/pressuring counts — that's Improper Concession (PPG §4.9). See [[conduct-infractions-overview]].
- Discussing outcome-dependent considerations (e.g. prizes) removes the ability to concede.

A conceding player who wasn't allowed to concede should be dropped unless there's a genuine reason (emergency, feeling unwell, discomfort with opponent) (§3.5). Prize splits are fine if they don't influence the result (TRP §6.4). Ties to [[dropping-from-tournament]]. — TRP (latest, 2026-06-10).
