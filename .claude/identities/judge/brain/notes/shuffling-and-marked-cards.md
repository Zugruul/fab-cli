Shuffling (TRP §5.11): shuffle = randomize so no player knows the card order. Decks must be shuffled at the start of every game and whenever instructed, then presented to the opponent to cut or shuffle. Recommended: multiple riffle + overhand shuffles then a cut. Pile counting / deterministic methods are NOT sufficient alone. If a player could see any card faces while shuffling, the deck isn't randomized and must be reshuffled.
- Failure to randomize = Insufficient Shuffling (PPG §3.8, Warning) — supervise and re-educate.
- Illegally randomizing an ordered set (e.g. shuffling in pitched cards, absent-mindedly mid-game) = Shuffling Error (PPG §2.6, IP2) because the state is irreversibly compromised.

Marked cards (TRP §5.13, PPG §3.7): a card/set is marked if distinguishable from the back/while face-down (reversed orientation, curl, bends, sleeve wear, mismatched sleeve hue/misprint). Players are responsible for keeping deck-cards unmarked. The Head Judge determines what's marked and may require immediate or next-round re-sleeving.
- Marked Cards infraction = Warning; have them re-sleeve/replace; in exceptional cases the HJ issues a proxy; a Cracked Bauble may substitute if no replacement is found (PPG §3.7).
- Upgrade to Game Loss if the HJ believes the player would have gained significant advantage from noticing the marking (§3.7).

Intentional marking or exploiting marks is Cheating (PPG §4.2), see [[conduct-infractions-overview]]. Ties to [[deck-checks]] and [[head-judge-authority]]. — TRP/PPG (latest, 2026-06-10).
