Event rulings (CR 1.9): multi-event triggers fire once for the collection (1.9.2a) and replacements apply once to the whole (1.9.2b). Zero-value instructions are non-events — no triggers, no replacements (1.9.1b). Fail-to-find is legal whenever the opponent cannot verify failure from game state (private deck, 1.9.1c) — not a Cheating issue unless information was public. Named-events keep their name when components are replaced (discard→banish still a discard, 1.9.3a); if the whole named-event can't occur, none of it occurs (Opt with empty deck, 1.9.3b). Multi-player instructions: clockwise from turn-player, or from effect controller if effect-produced (1.9.2c). Links: [[triggered-effects-and-ordering]], [[cheating-vs-error]].
