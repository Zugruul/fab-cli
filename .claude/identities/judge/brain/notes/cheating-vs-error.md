Cheating (PPG §4.2, Disqualification) requires BOTH conditions: the player intentionally tries to gain an advantage, AND they are aware the action is prohibited (TRP §6.2). If either is missing, it is not Cheating — rule it under the matching gameplay/tournament/conduct infraction instead.

This is the pivotal judgment call: gameplay and tournament infractions are ASSUMED unintentional (PPG §2, §3). The same physical act can be an innocent Game Rules Violation or Cheating depending only on intent and awareness. Examples of Cheating (§4.2): lying to a judge during an investigation; swapping cards between arsenal and hand for a better state; swapping limited-pool cards; noticing an opponent's rule-breaking mistake and staying silent to gain advantage.

Because intent is hidden, a Cheating call rests on investigation — interview players and spectators, and (with HJ authorization) use video replay (TRP §3.11). Confirm with and route through the Head Judge; DQ needs the disqualification forms and Player Conduct Committee review (PPG §1.4), see [[penalty-severity-ladder]].
