Priority determines who may act. Only one player has priority at a time; the holder is the "active player" (CR 1.11.2). Priority exists only in the Action Phase, never during the Close Step (CR 1.11.3).

Passing: the active player may play a card, activate an ability, or pass (CR 1.11.1). After playing/activating they REGAIN priority (CR 1.11.5). If all players pass in succession with a non-empty stack, the top layer resolves; if the stack is empty, the phase/step ends (CR 1.11.4a).

Resolving a layer (CR 5.3.1) proceeds: Check resolution → Static effects → Layer effects → Go again → Leave stack → Clear, then the turn-player gains priority (5.3.1, 5.3.7). Exception: an attack on top of the stack goes to the queue instead of resolving (5.3.1a) — see [[combat-chain-steps]].

Ruling relevance: a player who "already passed" has not lost the chance to respond until all players pass in succession; a substantial pause after regaining priority signals a completed action. No one has priority mid-resolution or during game state actions (1.11.5), see [[game-state-actions-and-death]]. Connects to [[triggered-effects-and-ordering]]. — CR (latest, 2026-06-10).
