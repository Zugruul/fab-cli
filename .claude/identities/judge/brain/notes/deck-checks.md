A deck-check verifies a player's cards against their accepted decklist (TRP §3.8). A REGULAR deck-check collects the deck after the player presents it (before they draw their opening hand) and returns it without guaranteeing order; it confirms cards aren't marked and were presented legally.

REL requirements (§3.8): regular deck-checks are REQUIRED at Professional, recommended at Competitive, and optional (TO's discretion) at Casual. Recommendation: check at least 10% of random players during Swiss before the play-offs begin. Extra checks may be done for investigation.

Timing (§3.8, §3.2): if the round is timed, give affected players extra time — the deck-check duration plus 3 minutes to reshuffle/present, capped at the original round length (see [[round-structure-and-timing]]).

The Head Judge may run a NON-regular deck-check on any player any time, even mid-game or between rounds (§3.8):
- Mid-game: the deck must be returned to exactly reinstate the pre-check game state.
- Between rounds: it isn't a presentation, so marked-card and presentation errors typically don't apply.

Mismatches map to PPG infractions (Decklist Error §3.4, Card-Pool Contents Error §3.5), see [[tournament-infractions-overview]] and [[decklists-registration]]. — TRP (latest, 2026-06-10).
