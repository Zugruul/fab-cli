**Assembly Module** (Mechanologist Action Item): "Crank — This enters the arena with a steam counter. At the start of your turn, destroy this unless you remove a steam counter from it." Crank itself (CR 8.3.29) is a static ability meaning "As this enters the arena, you may remove a steam counter from it. If you do, gain an action point."

Ruling: Assembly Module's own printed text gives it a steam counter AS it enters, and Crank's "as this enters the arena" clause applies at that same event window (CR 8.3.29a: "if a player removes a steam counter using crank, they are considered to have cranked"). So you CAN crank immediately on the turn Assembly Module enters — using its own entering counter — for an immediate action point, but doing so removes the very counter that would otherwise let it survive to your next turn's upkeep check. This is a real cost/benefit choice, not free value: crank now for tempo and lose the permanent at your next turn's start (since no counter remains to remove then), or decline to crank and keep Assembly Module around to activate its search ability at least once more, cranking (if a later effect adds another steam counter) on a subsequent turn instead.
