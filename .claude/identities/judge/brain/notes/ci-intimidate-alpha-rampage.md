**Alpha Rampage** (Brute Attack Action, Rhinar Specialization): "As an additional cost to play Alpha Rampage, discard a random card. When you attack with Alpha Rampage, intimidate." Note the printed text has no "target hero" clause. Intimidate (CR 8.5.10): "Intimidate is a discrete effect and delayed-triggered effect. To intimidate a player, they banish a random card from their hand face-down, and then at the beginning of the end phase, it returns to their hand." CR 8.5.10b: "If a player is not specified, the intimidate is a targeted effect."

Ruling: because Alpha Rampage's ability text says only "intimidate" with no player specified, the ability is a TARGETED effect per 8.5.10b — the controller must choose a legal target hero when the triggered ability is put on the stack (standard targeting rules apply, e.g. hero must be a legal target at that time). In standard 1v1 play there is only one opposing hero so the choice is trivial, but in any format with more than two heroes in play (team/multiplayer variants), the attacking player must actively choose WHICH opposing hero to intimidate — it is not automatically "the hero being attacked."
