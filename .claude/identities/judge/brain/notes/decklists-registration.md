Card-pool registration (decklist) is the recorded list of cards a player may use in a format (TRP §3.7). Required at ALL Competitive and Professional REL tournaments; optional at Casual (TO may require it). Specific formatting requirements are in TRP Appendix A.5.

Judge-relevant rules (§3.7):
- On submission the decklist becomes the TO's property. Illegal, illegible, or improperly formatted lists may be rejected.
- Once accepted, the player may NOT alter it.
- Submission deadline: before round 1 (constructed), end of deck construction (limited), or a TO-specified time. Late submission may incur penalties — see Tardiness/Decklist Error in [[tournament-infractions-overview]].
- Players may request to see their own decklist between matches (honor if logistically possible).

Privacy: lists should stay private to player and officials, except for coverage and for play-off opponents at Professional REL, where each play-off player in a constructed format is given their opponents' lists with time to review before game 1 (§3.7).

A list that is legal and reflects intent but mismatches the physical cards is a Card-Pool Contents Error, not a Decklist Error (PPG §3.5). Deck-checks verify cards against the list: [[deck-checks]]. Format sizes: [[official-formats]]. — TRP (latest, 2026-06-10).
