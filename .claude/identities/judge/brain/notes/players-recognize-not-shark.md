A note on how a PLAYER should use penalty knowledge (as opposed to a judge): learn the PPG only enough to RECOGNIZE a mis-applied penalty and to appeal it — never to weaponize the rules against an opponent.

Why: FAB explicitly punishes using rules knowledge to trap opponents. Rules Sharking (PPG §4.10, Warning; malicious pattern → Game Loss) covers preventing an opponent's legal interaction or leading them into an infraction — e.g. rushing "no blocks, no reactions" then blocking their reaction window, or baiting a missed trigger. Intentionally progressing the game to make an opponent miss their own trigger is also Rules Sharking (PPG §2.3). Methodical or pedantic play alone is NOT sharking unless intent is malicious (§4.10), and the Head Judge decides — see [[head-judge-authority]].

How to apply the recognition, not the shark: if a penalty looks wrong, appeal to the Head Judge BEFORE it's applied (only until then can you appeal) — [[judge-calls-and-appeals]]. Demanding a specific penalty for your opponent, or demanding the HJ before the first judge rules, is itself Unsporting Conduct (PPG §4.1). Overview: [[conduct-infractions-overview]]. — PPG (latest, 2026-06-10).
