Combat resolves an attack through ordered steps on the combat chain (CR 7.0.1). Knowing the exact step is how you rule reaction-window disputes.

Steps per chain link (CR 7.1–7.7):
1. Layer Step — attack sits on the stack; passes to the queue (7.1).
2. Attack Step — attack moves onto the chain as a chain link, becomes attacking, "attack" triggers fire; turn-player gets priority (7.2).
3. Defend Step — defending cards declared from hand/equipment; declaring is NOT playing them (no cost, no stack, no resolution abilities) (7.3.2). Defense reaction cards may NOT be declared here (7.3.2a).
4. Reaction Step — attacker plays attack reactions, defender plays defense reactions; a defense reaction becomes a defending card on resolution (7.4.2d).
5. Damage Step — if attack power > total defense, the target is hit for the difference; a hit-event is what "on hit" triggers key off (7.5.2, 7.5.5).
6. Resolution Step — chain link resolves, go again grants an action point, turn-player may play another attack (7.6).
7. Close Step — no priority; chain closes, cards clear/return, "combat chain closes" triggers fire (7.7).

While the chain is open you cannot play non-attack action cards/abilities except as instants (7.0.1a). Ties to [[priority-and-the-stack]] and [[triggered-effects-and-ordering]]. — CR (latest, 2026-06-10).
