A player may drop at any time by informing the Scorekeeper (TRP §3.6). Re-entry is only at the Head Judge's discretion.

Judge-relevant consequences (§3.6):
- No-shows are treated as conceding the match and should be dropped unless they tell the Scorekeeper before the next round's pairings are generated.
- Drop before the first pairings → treated as not having participated; not listed in final standings.
- Drop after a round's pairings are generated → treated as conceding that round's match before dropping.
- Drop during a limited portion → the player keeps all cards they correctly hold (opened, unopened, partially-drafted).
- Dropping early may forfeit participation prizes at the TO's discretion; a player who drops after being announced in the top cut is NOT replaced.
- No dropping in exchange for consideration — Bribery (TRP §6.4).

A Disqualification also removes the player from standings differently — see [[penalty-severity-ladder]]. Concession mechanics: [[draws-and-concessions]]. — TRP (latest, 2026-06-10).
