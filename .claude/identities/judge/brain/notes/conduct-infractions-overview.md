Conduct infractions cover general behavior and can be intentional OR unintentional (PPG §4). Many carry Disqualification; the first duty is always to de-escalate and ensure safety.

Taxonomy (default penalty):
- Unsporting Conduct (§4.1): Minor = Warning (mild offensive language, ignoring an official, kicking a chair); Major = Disqualification (slurs, harassment, making someone feel unsafe, wiping a card on the body). The Head Judge defines what counts — see [[head-judge-authority]].
- Cheating (§4.2, DQ): intentional + aware it's prohibited — see [[cheating-vs-error]].
- Aggressive Behavior (§4.3, DQ): physical harm or threat of it.
- Improperly Determining a Winner (§4.4, Game Loss): using anything but playing the game to decide a winner (e.g. a coin flip).
- Bribery (§4.5, DQ; ignorance→Game Loss) and Betting (§4.6, DQ; ignorance→Game Loss).
- Theft (§4.7, DQ).
- Stalling (§4.8, DQ) — see [[slow-play-vs-stalling]].
- Improper Concession (§4.9, Game Loss): asking for / pressuring a concession, or conceding after time+action — see [[draws-and-concessions]].
- Rules Sharking (§4.10, Warning): preventing an opponent's legal interaction or leading them into an infraction; malicious pattern → Game Loss. Methodical/pedantic play alone isn't sharking.
- Fraud (§4.11, DQ): misrepresenting identity/participation/results — see [[player-eligibility-and-identification]].
