Slow Play and Stalling are the SAME visible behavior split by intent — a core judge distinction.

Slow Play (PPG §3.3, Caution; TRP §5.7): taking longer than acceptable to make a decision that progresses the state, WITHOUT intent to gain advantage. Rule of thumb: if you can understand the state and find a line in the time the player takes for one decision, they're likely too slow (allow for instant-speed sequencing exceptions). Procedure: Caution + educate, then watch 1–2 decisions. Upgrade to Warning if it continues; to Game Loss after two Slow Play Warnings this tournament. Confirm by OBSERVING the game yourself — never penalize on spectator/player testimony alone, and don't grant extra time on mere accusation.

Stalling (PPG §4.8, Disqualification; TRP §6.6): INTENTIONALLY playing slowly to exploit the round time limit (e.g. slowing down while ahead near time). It's a conduct infraction, not a game-pace one.

The judge's job is to read intent, mirroring [[cheating-vs-error]]. Exceeding the start-of-game time limit is Slow Play (TRP §3.3, §5.7), see [[start-of-game-procedure]]. Pace disputes tie to [[round-structure-and-timing]]; overview in [[conduct-infractions-overview]] and [[tournament-infractions-overview]]. — PPG/TRP (latest, 2026-06-10).
